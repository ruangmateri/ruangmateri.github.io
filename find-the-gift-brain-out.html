6. 2 is the scanner the "brain" of the machine?............


Jawaban:

No, the scanner is not considered the "brain" of the machine. The brain of a machine refers to its central processing unit (CPU) or main processor, which performs calculations, executes instructions, and controls the overall functioning of the machine. The scanner, on the other hand, is a hardware device that captures and digitizes physical documents or images into a digital format. While the scanner is an important component in certain machines, such as multifunction printers or document management systems, it does not have the same level of processing capabilities as the machine's CPU.

Your lost brain Cells are always replaced. b. The brain feels pain when you have a new headache.​


Penjelasan:

Let's evaluate each statement:

1. The weight of the brain depends on intelligence.

- False (F)

2. A Jazz Musician has a dominant right-handed person has a dominant left brain.

- False (F)

3. When you play a game of chess, you use the left side of your brain.

- True (T)

4. Your lost brain cells are always replaced.

- False (F)

5. The brain feels pain when you have a new headache.

- False (F)

So, the correct answers are:

1. F

2. F

3. T

4. F

5. F

1. F (False): The weight of the brain does not depend on intelligence.

2. F (False): Being left-handed or right-handed is not directly related to brain dominance or intelligence in the context of being a jazz musician.

3. F (False): A left-handed person does not necessarily have a dominant left brain. Brain dominance is a complex and individualized trait that is not solely determined by handedness.

4. F (False): When playing a game of chess, both sides of the brain are involved, not just the left side.

5. F (False): Lost brain cells are not always replaced. The brain has a limited ability to regenerate cells, and the replacement of brain cells is a complex process that varies depending on the specific circumstances.

b. F (False): The brain itself does not feel pain. Headaches and other forms of physical discomfort associated with the head are typically caused by other structures such as nerves and blood vessels. The brain lacks pain receptors.

Explanation

1. This statement is wrong because brain weight does not depend on a person's level of intelligence. Brain weight can vary between individuals, but there is no direct correlation between brain weight and intelligence.

2. This statement is wrong. there is no direct relationship between playing jazz music and right brain dominance. The fact that someone is right-handed or left-handed does not determine their brain dominance or intelligence.

3. This statement is wrong. A left-handed individual does not necessarily have a dominant left brain. Brain dominance is a complex and individual feature and is not determined solely by hand usage habits.

4. This statement is wrong. When playing chess, both sides of the brain are involved. The cognitive activities and strategies required in playing chess involve brain work in both parts, both the left and right sides.

5. This statement is wrong. Lost brain cells are not always replaced. some parts of the brain have a limited ability to regenerate their own cells. Brain cell replacement procedures are varied and complex depending on the case and other factors involved.

b. This statement is wrong. The brain itself does not feel pain. Headaches and physical discomfort associated with the head are usually caused by other structures such as nerves and blood vessels. The brain has no pain receptors
