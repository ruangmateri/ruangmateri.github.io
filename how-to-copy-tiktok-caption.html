6. 1.What is caption? 2. What is the importance of caption? 3. What are the characteristics of a good caption? 4. What are types of caption? 5. How to make a good caption? 6. What is the text structure of a caption? 7. Please make a conclusion of a caption!​


Jawaban:

1. Caption refers to a short description or text that accompanies an image, photograph, or illustration. It provides context, explanation, or additional information to enhance the viewer's understanding or appreciation of the visual content.

2. The importance of a caption lies in its ability to complement and enhance the visual content it accompanies. Captions can serve several purposes, such as providing necessary details, clarifying the subject matter, evoking emotions, or delivering key messages. They help capture the viewer's attention and create a more engaging and informative experience.

3. Characteristic of a good caption includes:

- Conciseness: Captions should be brief and to the point, conveying the essential information without being overly wordy.

- Relevance: A good caption should be directly related to the visual content and provide relevant context or details.

- Clarity: Captions should be clear and easily understood, avoiding overly technical or ambiguous language.

- Engaging: An effective caption captures attention and engages the viewer by being interesting, creative, or thought-provoking.

- Accuracy: Captions must accurately represent the visual content and provide correct information.

4. There are different types of captions, including:

- Descriptive Captions: Focus on providing factual information about the visual content, such as identifying subjects, locations, or events.

- Explanatory Captions: Offer explanations or interpretations of the visual content, helping viewers understand the context or meaning.

- Narrative Captions: Tell a story or present a sequence of events related to the visual content.

- Humorous or Creative Captions: Use humor, wit, or creativity to add an entertaining or distinctive element to the visual content.

5. To create a good caption, you can follow these steps:

- Understand the visual content: Analyze the image or illustration to grasp its key elements, message, or mood.

- Identify the purpose: Determine what information or emotion you want to convey through the caption.

- Keep it concise: Craft a brief but impactful caption that gets straight to the point.

- Be descriptive and relevant: Provide relevant details, explain the context, or highlight the main subject of the visual content.

- Be creative (if appropriate): Use humor, puns, or creativity to make the caption more engaging, but ensure it remains relevant and respectful.

6. The text structure of a caption is usually concise and straightforward. It can vary depending on the content and purpose but often follows a simple format of introducing the subject, providing additional information or context, and concluding with a clear message or takeaway.

7. In conclusion, a caption serves as an important complement to visual content by providing context, information, or emotional cues. A good caption is concise, relevant, engaging, accurate, and enhances the viewer's understanding or appreciation of the visual content. It should capture attention, convey the intended message, and make the overall experience more enjoyable and informative.

12. how to copy a document


Maksudnya cara menyalin dokumen gitu kah? Step 1: Open an existing Word document or start a new document and type your text. You can find Word using Cortana or search for it in the search bar or Windows list of programs. 

 

Step 2: To copy text, it will need to be selected or highlighted with the mouse first. When the mouse pointer is moved over a text area, the pointer will change from an arrow to a ‘text select’ or ‘I-beam’ icon.

 

 

Step 3: Select your text so that it’s highlighted.

 

Step 4: Copy the text by clicking on the copy icon at the left-hand side of the formatting ribbon.

 

 

Step 5: Use the mouse to move the cursor to where you wish to paste the copied text.

 

Step 6: Click Paste to insert the copied text in its new place in your text.

 

 

Step 8: Your text will now be duplicated in the new position in your document.

 

Step 9: Once text is highlighted, it’s also possible to copy and paste it using a keyboard shortcut instead of the mouse, which some people find easier. To copy, press and hold Ctrl (the control key) on the keyboard and then press the C on the keyboard. To paste, press and hold down Ctrl and then press V.

17. HOW TO OPERATE PHOTO COPY MACHINE BESERTA ARTINYA


A photocopy machine is one of the most important pieces of equipment in any business. As a business employee, you may be responsible for making hundreds of photocopies on a daily basis. You should not be embarrassed if you do not have experience with a photocopier, however. Many people view photocopy machines as large foreign objects. The following is a quick tutorial that can having you making copies in less than two minutes:

Turn on the Photocopier

The first step you will take in using the photocopier is making sure it is powered on a plugged up. First, check for plugs, and make sure that the copier’s cord is plugged into a nearby outlet. Next, look for the power button. Most copiers have the power button on top, but you may find the switch on the side. If you find that the unit is already on but is not responding, then it may be in “sleep” mode. In that case, you can press the “copy” button to see if it responds to that.

Before moving to the next step, you will want to make sure that the copier has paper in it. The paper tray will be located either on the front or the side of the device. Fill the tray with paper if you notice that it is empty or the level is low.

Put Your Document on the Copier

The next step is placing your document on the copier. You will lift the photocopier’s cover and place your document on the glass surface. You will want the front of the document facing downward on the glass. You will also want to place the document the way the guide marks tell you. You will see the guide marks once you lift the cover. After you have properly placed your document, you can close the copier cover.

Select the Number of Copies

You will need to select the number of copies that you want the copier to make. The unit will have a digital number display and a set of plus and minus keys so that you can change the quantity. Some models have numeric keys for this same function.

Select the Color Preference

Next, you will want to tell the copier whether you would like color copies or black and white copies. The unit will have a dedicated button for this selection. Press the button to make your changes.

Select Paper Size

After you select the color preference, you will want to select the correct paper size. 8.5 x 11 inches is the standard paper size. If you need to use a special paper size, then you will have to feed the paper into the tray.

Press the Copy Button

Finally, you will press the “copy” button to print your copies. Some units have the copy button labeled as a start button. Once you press the copy button, your copies will start printing. You may stand in front of the copier while is it printing so that you catch any errors immediately.
