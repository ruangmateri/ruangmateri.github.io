6. tuliskan tex tentang im pproud of indonesia


I am proud of Indonesia

Indonesia is a big country. Located between two continents, Asia and Australia, and between two oceans, the Pacific and the Indian. Indonesia is the largest archipelagic country in the world. There are more than 17 thousand islands in Indonesia.

There are many seas in Indonesia, Java sea, Bali sea, Arafuru sea, Banda sea, Timor sea and others. Indonesia also has many straits, such as the Sunda Strait, between the islands of Java and Sumatra, the Strait of Bali between Bali and Lombok, and others.

Indonesia has many islands. The biggest ones are Papua, Kalimantan, Sumatra, Sulawesi and Java. Among the five islands, Java is the smallest, but the most populated. There we can meet everyone from all over Indonesia. Indonesia also has many mountains. Many of them are still active and can erupt at any time. We call it a volcano, like Sinabung and Merapi in Sumatra, Merapi on Java and Lokon in South Sulawesi. When the mountains erupted, hot lava came out of the bowels of the earth.

Indonesia is located on the equator. Indonesia is a tropical country. The sun shines brightly every day, so it's mostly hot. In Indonesia there are two seasons, the rainy season and the dry season.

The land is fertile. Farmers grow many kinds of vegetables and fruits. They also plant coconut. Indonesians eat lots of vegetables such as spinach, carrots, long beans, teron, cabbage, cucumber, tomatoes, onions, garlic, chili, kale and others.

We also grow many kinds of fruits. We call it local fruit, such as guava, banana, rambutan, durian, mangosteen, soursop, papaya, pineapple and zalacca. Some people call it the fruit of snake skin. All tastes good, sweet and runny. We eat it fresh. We also make delicious juices from these fruits. We also dry many kinds of fruit for chips.

Indonesia is also rich in spices such as pepper, coriander, ginger, clove, cinnamon, turmeric, galangal, lemongrass, bay leaves and others. We use it to cook very spicy Indonesian food. People also make healthy drinks from the spices. The drinks are beneficial for our health.

Many kinds of fish, from small to large live in the sea of ​​Indonesia. Most of us eat fish. We also eat shrimp and various shellfish. But we don't eat big fish, like dolphins and sharks. We protect them. We also protect turtles.

In Indonesia, we also raise animals for their chicks such as cows, goats, pigs. We get beef from beef, mutton from goat and pork from pork. Some ethnic groups even eat horse meat. The milk we consume comes from cows. Of course we also raise chickens. They gave us meat and eggs.

I want to let the world know that I am so proud of my mother. I want to shout it from the rooftops. So listen up world as I’m going to brag about some of the wonderful accomplishments that she has achieved throughout her lifetime to date.

Firstly she gave birth to me. Wow! Now that has to be something to shout about. She gave me the greatest gift that can be given to any human – and that is life. She carried me for nine months before I gasped for air and then loved me throughout my childhood, on through my teenage years and well into adulthood.

Of her childhood I know little except that at some point her mother and father separated and divorced and with her other siblings was deposited into a home with strangers. I can only imagine the pain experienced for a child to be plucked from their surroundings and placed into the care of strangers.

At the tender age of 14 she left school and took up employment. In 1957 she was married and took up residence with my father in a two bedroom fibro house at Peakhurst South, a southern suburb in the city of Sydney Australia at the age of 18, and then gave birth to me when she was 20 years of age.

When I was six years old she decided that she wanted to learn the piano and at the same time I was included in the equation. This is when I took my first piano lessons and developed my love for music.

Throughout my childhood, although a stay at home mum, she was always busying herself by teaching Sunday school, taking on piano students, teaching Scripture at our local school and even took on the responsibility of a foster child when I was nine.

32. What are the five pillars of faith and give explanations of each pillars!


Jawaban:

Shahada (Faith)

The declaration of faith in one God (Allah) and His messenger (peace be upon him).

Salah (Prayer)

The ritual prayer required of every Muslim five times a day throughout their lifetime.

Zakat (Almsgiving)

The act of giving a portion of a Muslim’s wealth to those in need throughout their lifetime.

Sawm (Fasting)

The act of fasting during the holy month of Ramadan.

Hajj (Pilgrimage)

The sacred pilgrimage to Mecca required of every Muslim at least once in their lifetime if it is within their means.
