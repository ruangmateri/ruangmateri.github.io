2. write a movie review about Parasite (South Korean movie) Thank youu​


Jawaban:

Parasite, directed by Bong Joon-ho, is an exceptional South Korean film that captivates audiences from start to finish. Seamlessly blending genres of dark comedy, drama, and thriller, this masterful work presents a thought-provoking exploration of the stark class divide that exists in society.

The film revolves around two families from different social backgrounds: the affluent Parks and the impoverished Kims. The Kims, struggling to make ends meet, cunningly infiltrate the lives of the Parks by securing employment within their luxurious household. What unfolds is a tale of deception, secrets, and unexpected consequences, as the stark contrast between the haves and the have-nots is laid bare.

The screenplay is brilliantly written, filled with sharp wit, social commentary, and moments of gripping tension. Bong Joon-ho's direction is impeccable, meticulously crafting each scene to enhance the film's themes. The cinematography expertly captures the stark visual contrast between the cramped living conditions of the Kims and the sprawling opulence of the Parks' residence.

One of the film's greatest strengths lies in its exceptional ensemble cast. Song Kang-ho delivers a standout performance as Mr. Kim, showcasing his versatility as an actor with a nuanced portrayal of a desperate and resourceful father. Cho Yeo-jeong impresses as Mrs. Park, perfectly capturing the obliviousness and naivety of the privileged class.

Parasite excels in its ability to seamlessly shift tones, effortlessly transitioning from lighthearted comedic moments to gut-wrenching scenes of suspense. This tonal balance adds layers of complexity to the narrative, constantly keeping the audience engaged and on the edge of their seats.

What makes Parasite truly remarkable is its unflinching exploration of socioeconomic inequality. Bong Joon-ho deftly exposes the harsh realities faced by those on the lower rungs of society, while also highlighting the moral ambiguities that arise when one's circumstances push them to desperate measures. The film challenges our preconceptions and forces us to confront the uncomfortable truths about privilege, greed, and the lengths one may go to escape poverty.

Parasite's brilliance extends beyond its storytelling. The film's meticulous production design, atmospheric score, and seamless editing all contribute to its immersive experience. Every aspect of this cinematic gem is crafted with such precision that it becomes impossible to look away, even during its most shocking and intense moments.

In conclusion, Parasite is an absolute triumph of filmmaking, deserving of every accolade it has received. It serves as a compelling reminder of the deep-rooted issues of inequality within our society while delivering an enthralling and unpredictable narrative. Bong Joon-ho's genius shines through in every frame, leaving a lasting impact on its viewers. Parasite is a cinematic masterpiece that should not be missed.

17. Change this sentence into negative form. "Lala really likes watching korean movie".​


Negative form:

(-) "Lala does not really like watching korean movie"

Pembahasan Present Tense

Digunakan untuk menyatakan Kegiatan yg dilakukan secara berulang, rutin, kebiasaan dan hal2 bersifat umum/fakta.

Rumus Present Tense

(+) S + V1 (-s/es) + O/C

(-) S + do/does + not + V1 + O/C

(?) do/does + S + V1 + O/C?

Kalimat Present Tense

(+) "Lala really likes watching korean movie"

(-) "Lala does not really like watching korean movie"

(?) “does Lala really like watching korean movie"?

Catatan:

Jika Subjek tunggal

kalimat positive menggunakan Verb+s/es kalimat negative dan interrogative menggunakan does

Jika Subjek jamak

kalimat positive menggunakan Verb-1 atau kalimat negative dan interrogative menggunakan do
