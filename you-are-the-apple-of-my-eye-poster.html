10. Describe clearly by using your English Language among these idioms: a. He was a bad egg b. You are the apple of my eyes c. Blow off steam


a. He was a bad egg

Meaning that someone who does bad things or someone who is not nice. It's the opposite of the idiom "the good egg" which means that someone is a kind person or a nice person.

Let's say that your neighbour is a rude guy and always does bad things, you can say that "he's a bad egg."

b. You are the apple of my eyes

Meaning that someone is very important or special to you or someone you love most or the sort.

If you say that Anna is "the apple of your eyes", you mean that she is someone who is important to you.

c. Blow off steam

Meaning to air or relieve one's pent-up feelings by loud talk or vigorous activity.

If you say that someone was "blowing off steam" when they shouted at you, you mean that they were so upset they just had to scream to sound off their feelings.
