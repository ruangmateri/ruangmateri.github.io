8.)Elephant Size As explained above, it turns out that African elephants are bigger than Asian elephants. Yes, African elephants weigh between 2268 and 6350 kilograms and reach 4 meters in height. While the weight range of Asian elephants is in the range of 2041 to 4990 kilograms with a height reaching 3 meters. Proboscis on the Elephant The proboscis in this elephant is an extension of the upper lip and nose. The trunk of an elephant has various functions that are vital for the life of an elephant. Yes, the elephant's trunk is used to grasp, breathe, to eat, that is to put food in the mouth, to drink, to cleanse itself from dust, to sniff, lift, communicate, as a protective device and of course as a sensing device. To support these vital functions, the elephant's trunk is estimated to be equipped with 100,000 muscles and tendons. This makes the elephant's trunk not only very flexible, but also strong. This elephant trunk can also be elongated, you know. It also can tighten or contract, and can be moved in all directions according to the will of the elephant. Elephant Ears Not only his body is big, some other elephant's body parts are also big like ears. One of the characteristics of this elephant is the size of its large ears. Its size can reach one sixth of its body size and has a main function in the cooling process. This elephant ear contains a network of blood vessels with a very small size and so extensive. Warm blood flow when circulating in the tissue will cool as a result of environmental influences outside the body of an elephant. This can occur because the layer of skin on an elephant is very thin, so contact between the blood with a cooler outside environment temperature can occur. Based on this function, the ear of an elephant whose habitat is closer to the equator will be larger than an elephant whose habitat is further away from the equator. The more the habitat to the equator, the more heat must be removed from the body of an elephant. Therefore elephants have wider ear shapes.
