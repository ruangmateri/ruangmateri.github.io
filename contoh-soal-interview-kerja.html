Penjelasan

Job interview adalah wawancara yang dilakukan oleh karyawan atau manajer personalia terhadap pelamar pekerjaan. Bagian personalia menanyakan hal-hal tentang data diri pelamar dan beberapa pertanyaan terkait posisi yang akan ditempati oleh pelamar. Dalam kesempatan itu, pelamar juga dapat menceritakan keahlian dan pengalamannya sebagai bahan pertimbangan untuk bagian personalia, sekaligus menunjukkan bahwa yang bersangkutan pantas berada di posisi tersebut.

1. What is a job interview? (Apakah wawancara kerja itu?)

a. an interview to find information

b. an interview to find the crime suspect

c. an interview to select an applicant (wawancara untuk menyeleksi pelamar)

2. Who interviews the applicants in job interview? (Siapakah yang mewawancara pelamar pada wawancara kerja)

a. HR Officer (bagian personalia)

b. Sales Marketing

c. Receptionist

3. What is applicant? (Apakah pelamar itu?

a. The one whom interviewing

b. The one whom welcoming

c. The one whom applying (orang yang melamar)

4. Where can we find the job advertisement? (Dimanakah dapat kita temukan iklan pekerjaan?)

a. In the newspaper (di koran)

b. In the phone

c. In the computer

5. Who can apply the job? (Siapakah yang bisa melamar pekerjaan?)

a. All people that fit in the qualifications (semua orang yang cocok dengan persyaratan)

b. All people that hate the position

c. All people that miss the job information

6. What are the things in job advertisement? (Apa saja yang terdapat pada iklan pekerjaan?)

a. Position

b. Qualification

c. Both a and b (Kedua jawaban a dan b, yaitu posisi dan persyaratan)

7. What are going to be happened in the job interview? (Apa yang akan terjadi dalam wawancara kerja?)

a. Asking and answering related to the job position (menanyakan dan menjawab hal-hal berkaitan dengan posisi pekerjaan tersebut)

b. Just chatting

c. Discussing about politic

8. What do the applicant bring in the job interview? (Apa yang pelamar bawa ke wawancara kerja?)

a. Final thesis

b. Curriculum Vitae (Data diri)

c. Nothing

9. What do the applicant do before having the job interview? (Apa yang pelamar lakukan sebelum wawancara kerja?)

a. Sending flowers and gift

b. Sending best wishes

c. Sending job application letter (mengirimkan surat lamaran pekerjaan)

10. How should the applicant be during the job interview? (Bagaimana seharusnya pelamar bersikap selama wawancara kerja?)

a. The applicant should answer the questions well. (pelamar harus menjawab pertanyaan-pertanyaan dengan baik.)

b. The applicant should not listen the questions well.

c. The applicant should ignore the questions.

21. contoh job interview jurusan APHP​


Jawaban:

Pertanyaan Interview Bahasa Inggris

Okay, Please introduce yourself briefly? (Baiklah, Tolong perkenalkan diri anda secara singkat?)

Ini adalah pertanyaan paling mendasar yang biasa disodorkan dalam wawancara dengan menggunakan Bahasa Inggris. Artinya sederhana, yaitu meminta kamu untuk memperkenalkan dirimu secara singkat saja. Namun, ada sesuatu yang digali dari seorang yang mewawancara dari pertanyaan ini, yaitu tentang bagaimana kamu bisa membawa diri dan mengenalkan diri kamu secara baik dan benar. Berikut ini contoh dialognya:

You: My name is Rahman, I am twenty five years old. I have graduated from Gadjah Mada University, majoring in Computer Science. My Activity right now is writing on my Personal English Blog, I am also active in several communities that related to my background.

Anda: Nama saya Rahman, umur saya 25 tahun. Saya lulus kuliah dari Universitas Gadjah Mada, jurusan Komputer Sains. Aktivitas saya saat ini adalah menulis di blog berbahasa inggris pribadi saya, saya juga aktif di beberapa komunitas yang berhubungan dengan latar belakang saya.

Perkenalkan diri kamu dan bisa sedikit menyangkutkan dengan hal yang berkaitan dengan posisi yang ingin kamu lamar. Bisa juga ditambahkan dengan keterampilan yang diperlukan oleh pekerjaan impianmu tersebut.

What department do you apply and why do you interested on it? (Posisi apa yang anda lamar dan kenapa anda tertarik melamar posisi itu?)

Selanjutnya, setelah mengenalkan diri kamu secara singkat, biasanya pertanyaan selanjutnya dalam wawancara Bahasa Inggris adalah mengenai bagian apa yang kamu lamar dan mengapa kamu tertarik dengan posisi tersebut. Pertanyaan ini guna mencari tahu, apa motivasi kamu tentang pekerjaan yang ingin diambil. Apakah kamu serius untuk mengambilnya atau hanya sekedar main-main saja. Adapun contoh dialognya seperti berikut ini:

You: I want to apply for the System Analyst position, I am interested working on that position because that is my passion and I am also love working with computers since I was a kid.

Anda: Saya ingin melamar di posisi System Analyst, Saya tertarik bekerja pada posisi tersebut karena saya itulah semangat saya dan saya juga suka bekerja dengan komputer sejak saya masih kecil.

Kamu harus menjelaskan bagian apa yang kamu lamar dan alasan yang membuat kamu tertarik pada posisi tersebut. Berikan alasan pendukung yang akan membuat pewawancara yakin bahwa kamu pantas ada di posisi tersebut.

23. contoh dialog interview lamaran kerja sebagai pelayan​


Jawaban:

Interviewer: Good morning. Please have a seat. I will be conducting the interview for the waiter position. First, please introduce yourself.

Applicant: Thank you. My name is John. I have previous experience working as a waiter in a well-known restaurant for three years.

Interviewer: That's great, John. What sparked your interest in the waiter position at our company?

Applicant: I am interested in the position because I enjoy providing excellent customer service and creating a memorable dining experience. I have a passion for hospitality and interacting with people, and I believe that working as a waiter allows me to showcase my skills in a fast-paced and dynamic environment.

Interviewer: Excellent. Can you tell me about your previous experience as a waiter? What were your responsibilities and achievements?

Applicant: During my time as a waiter, I was responsible for taking orders, serving food and beverages, and ensuring customer satisfaction. I developed strong communication and multitasking skills, handling busy shifts while maintaining a high level of service. I also received positive feedback from customers for my friendly demeanor and attention to detail.

Interviewer: That's impressive, John. In this role, you will encounter various challenges, such as handling difficult customers or managing time during busy periods. How do you handle such situations?

Applicant: In challenging situations, I remain calm and composed. I believe in active listening and empathy, so I try to understand the concerns of the customers and address them professionally. Time management is crucial, and I prioritize tasks effectively to ensure smooth operations even during busy times.

Interviewer: Good to know, John. Lastly, do you have any questions for us about the position or the company?

Applicant: Yes, I would like to know more about the company's values and the opportunities for growth and development within the organization.

Interviewer: We value teamwork, excellence, and providing exceptional customer experiences. As for growth opportunities, we have a structured training program and a clear career path for our employees.

Applicant: That sounds promising. I look forward to the chance to contribute to the team and grow within the company.

Interviewer: Thank you for your time, John. We will be in touch with you soon regarding the next steps of the hiring process.

Applicant: Thank you. I appreciate the opportunity to interview with your company. Have a great day!

Interviewer: You're welcome, John. Have a great day too!

29. interview wawancarai teman tentang perkerjaan dlam BHS inggris​


Jawaban:

Berikut adalah contoh pertanyaan dan jawaban dalam bahasa Inggris untuk wawancara teman tentang pekerjaan:

Interviewer: Can you please introduce yourself and tell us about your current job position?

Teman: Certainly! My name is [Nama Teman]. Currently, I work as a software engineer at a technology company. I have been in this role for the past two years and have gained extensive experience in developing and maintaining software applications.

Interviewer: What are your responsibilities in your current job?

Teman: As a software engineer, my responsibilities include writing code, debugging and fixing issues, conducting software testing, and collaborating with cross-functional teams such as designers and product managers to develop high-quality software products. I also participate in code reviews and contribute to the overall improvement of our development processes.

Interviewer: What motivated you to pursue a career in the field of software engineering?

Teman: I have always been fascinated by technology and how it can solve real-world problems. The rapid advancements in software development intrigued me, and I wanted to be a part of innovating and creating solutions through coding. I enjoy the challenge of solving complex problems and the continuous learning opportunities that come with being a software engineer.

Interviewer: Can you describe a project that you have worked on that you are particularly proud of?

Teman: Certainly! One project that I am proud of is when I was involved in developing a mobile application for a start-up company. 40. contoh kalimat job interview open dan close dalam wawancara kerja dalam bahasa ingris?


Jawaban:

Open =

A: “Good morning, I am here for my interview.”

B: “Hello, nice to meet you. I am Mr. William. Have any trouble finding the place?”

A: “No problem.”

B: “So why don’t you tell me why you are interested in changing positions?”

A: “Unfortunately, our company is shutting down due to the economy.”

B: “What would you consider your strengths?”

A: “I am probably best at researching for marketing purposes.”

B: “What is your biggest weakness?”

A: “I tend to get bored easily and so love to keep myself challenged.”

B: “We have a position where that could work out well.”

A: “Great!”

close =

A: “Good morning, John. I am Mike.”

B: “Good morning.”

A: “How are you doing?”

B: “I am doing fine. Thank you.”

A: “How was the traffic coming over here?”

B: “I am so glad that the traffic was light this morning. No traffic jam and no accidents.”

A: “That is good. John, let’s start the interview. Are you ready?”

B: “Yes, I am.”

A: “First of all, let me properly introduce myself. I am the Finance Department Manager. As you know there is an open position in my department, and I need to fill this position as soon as possible.”

B: “Please, tell me a little bit about the position.”

 

A: “It is an entry-level position. The new employee will have to work closely with the Accounting department. He will also have to deal with the bank on a daily basis.”

B: “What type of qualifications do you require?”

A: “I require a four-year college degree in Finance. Some working experience would be helpful.”

B: “What kind of experience are you looking for?”

A: “Doing office work is good. However, since this is an entry-level position, I do not require a lot of experience. I am willing to train the new person.”

B: “That is great!”

A: “John, tell me a little bit about yourself.”

B: “I was a student at West Coast University, and I just graduated with a Bachelor degree in Finance. I have been working part-time as a payroll clerk the last two years.”

A: “What are you looking for in a job?”

B: “The job should help me see what Finance is all about. I have learned a lot of Finance theories at school, and now it is time for me to put them into practice.”

A: “Anything else?”

B: “I also hope that it will help me grow in my field.”

A: “What are your strengths? Why should I hire you?”

B: “I am a hard-working person and a fast learner. I am very eager to learn, and I get along fine with people.”

A: “Ok. Now, let me ask you a few quick questions. You do not mind working long hours, do you?”

B: “No, I don’t mind.”

A: “Can you handle pressure?”

B: “Yes, I can. When I was going to school, I took quite a few courses each semester while working at least twenty hours every week. And, I handled that situation very well.”

A: “Do you still have any questions for me?”

B: “No, I think I have a pretty good understanding of the job. I believe that I can handle it with ease, and I hope to have the opportunity to work with you.”

A: “John, nice meeting you. Thank you for coming.”

B: “Nice meeting you too. Thank you for seeing me.”

A: “Will let you know about the result later.”

B: “Waiting for good news from you.”
