31. How does the octopus run away from its Enemies?​


Jawaban:

The octopus has several ways to escape from its enemies. One of its main strategies is to use its ability to camouflage itself by changing color and texture to blend in with its surroundings. This makes it difficult for predators to spot it. Additionally, octopuses are able to quickly jet away by expelling water out of their mantles, which propels them quickly away from danger. They can also use their flexible and strong arms to squeeze into tight spaces and cracks as a way to escape. Moreover, they're able to release a cloud of ink to disorient and blind their predators.

Octopus is classified as a mollusk animal. The main habitat for animals from this class of cephalopods is coral. In general, there are as many as 289 species of octopuses, of which one third are cephalopods, namely animals with legs on their heads. This octopus has weapons to protect and escape from its enemies, namely with ink bags, camouflage and severing arms.
