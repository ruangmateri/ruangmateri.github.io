The floor is very dirty. Why don’t you (it / clean)? d. My father’s car is not working very well. He (it/service) tomorrow. e. Many young people today (their nose / pierce). f. She has got a bad toothache. She (it / remove) in a week. g. Summers are very hot in Spain. Many people (swimming pools / build) in their gardens. h. My office needs a change. I (it/decorate) in the spring. i. I (it / redecorate) in the spring. (my house / clean) once a week. j. They (their computers / repair) today.


PASSIVE CAUSATIVE  

a. I have lost my key. I’ll have to have/get another key made.  

b. Your hair is a mess. You should have your hair cut.

c. The floor is very dirty. Why don’t you have it cleaned?

d. My father’s car is not working very well. He will have/get it serviced tomorrow.  

e. Many young people today have their nose pierced.

f. She has got a bad toothache. She'll have it removed in a week.

g. Summers are very hot in Spain. Many people have swimming pools built in their gardens.

h. My office needs a change. I'll have it decorated in the spring.

i. I have my house cleaned once a week.

j. They'll their computers repaired today.

-----------

EXPLANATION

To have something done adalah tujuan dari kalimat passive causative.

Causative verb adalah kata kerja yang menyebabkan sesuatu terjadi.

Causative sentence adalah kalimat yang menggunakan causative verb di mana subject kalimat bukanlah yang melakukan aksi secara langsung tetapi menyebabkan aksi terjadi dengan cara membuat orang lain melakukannya.

Contoh kalimat causative:

I have him carry my books → saya menyuruh/membuat dia membawakan buku saya.

Causative verb yang biasa digunakan adalah let, make, have, dan get.

Passive causative adalah kalimat causative dalam bentuk pasif. Pada passive causative, objek dari causative verb nya bukan orang yang mengerjakan perintah tetapi benda yang dikenai pekerjaan.

Contoh passive causative:

I have my book carried (by him) → saya membuat buku saya dibawakan (oleh dia).

Konstruksi passive causative adalah:

S + causative verb + O (something) + past participle (v₃)
