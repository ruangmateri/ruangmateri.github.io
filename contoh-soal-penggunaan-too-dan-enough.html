Jawaban:

TOO

1. he's too strong for his age

2. its too heavy

3. its too dark down here

4. she's too beautiful to be true

5. they're too noisy

ENOUGH

1. he is not smart enough to be paired with

2. this light is not bright enough to be able to see well in this dark space

3. you have enough money to buy it, want to buy it now?

4. you're not good enough to have friends like them!

5. you haven't tried enough, it's not your best effort

Jawaban:

TOO artinya terlalu atau sesuatu yang berlebihan. Sedangkan Enough artinya cukup.

Too dapat dibedakan menjadi 2 ( too much & too many )

TOO MUCH : Uncountable (tidak bisa dihitung)TOO MANY : Countable (dapat dihitung)

EXAMPLE 'TOO' :

You put sugar too much

I'll giving you too many meals

Audrey too young to protect my son

My uncle is too slowly to driving a car

The exam is too hard for me

EXAMPLE 'ENOUGH'

I don't have money enough to buy it

Never enough to say it just by words

19. bagaimana penggunaan too(terlalu) dan enough(cukup) ?


Contoh: 
- too = iPhone is too expensive for me
- enough = I feel not good enough today too + adjective + infinitive
biasanya 'too' menunjukkan hasil negatif, bandingkan kalimat berikut
- the box is too heavy, i can not lift it
- the box is very heavy, but i can lift it

enough + noun + infinitive
contoh : i don't have enough money to buy car

adjective + enough + infinitive
contoh : my mother is old enough

30. cara penggunaan enough dan too


Enough ditempatkan setelah adjective (kata sifat) atau adverb (kata keterangan).
contohAm I qualified enough? (adjective)
  Rumus too : too + adjective or adverb
contoh:
He is too old to play with us. (Dia terlalu tua untuk bermain bersama kami.)
enough = artinya cukup
ex : i'm full, enough food I eat today.
too = terlalu
ex : he is too lazy

37. Buat masing2 3 contoh kalimat menggunakan 1. Adjective enough 2. Adverb enough 3. Too adjective 4. Too adverb​


1. Adjective enough :

• The water isn't cold enough for me to swim in.

•This dress isn't long enough for the wedding.

•The cake wasn't sweet enough for my taste.

2. Adverb enough :

• He didn't run fast enough to catch the bus.

• She didn't speak loudly enough for the audience to hear.

• They didn't arrive early enough to get good seats.

3. Too adjective:

• The coffee is too hot to drink right now.

• The movie was too long and boring for me to enjoy.

• The music is too loud for me to concentrate.

4. Too adverb:

• She drove too fast on the highway and got a ticket.

• He talks too much during meetings and it's annoying.

• They danced too wildly and got kicked out of the club.
